## Strategic Case History

Mark Roher has been involved in some of the most significant bankruptcy and financial fraud cases in South Florida history. The following highlights represent a cross-section of his career accomplishments:

### Rothstein Recovery Efforts

Navigated the intricate legal fallout of the **Scott Rothstein Ponzi scheme**, one of the largest financial fraud cases in Florida history. Mark Roher represented parties in complex clawback actions, fraudulent transfer litigation, and asset recovery proceedings arising from the collapse of the Rothstein Rosenfeldt Adler law firm. His work in this matter required deep expertise in **11 U.S.C. sections 544, 547, and 548** (avoidance powers) and coordination with the court-appointed trustee.

### Madoff-Related Recovery Actions

Provided strategic counsel in matters connected to the **Bernard Madoff investment fraud**, including representation of investors and feeder fund participants in recovery actions within the Southern District of Florida. These cases demanded a thorough understanding of **SIPA (Securities Investor Protection Act)** proceedings and the interplay between federal bankruptcy law and securities regulation.

### Corporate Restructuring

Successfully guided multiple **retail, hospitality, and real estate entities** through the Chapter 11 reorganization process to maintain operational continuity, preserve jobs, and maximize value for all stakeholders. Mark Roher's approach to corporate restructuring emphasizes practical solutions, creditor negotiation, and court-approved plans of reorganization that balance debtor rehabilitation with creditor recovery.

### Complex Fraud Defense

Defended clients against **multimillion-dollar clawback claims** in federal court, including actions brought by bankruptcy trustees and liquidation trusts. His defense strategies have included challenging the elements of fraudulent transfer under both federal and Florida state law (Florida Uniform Fraudulent Transfer Act, Fla. Stat. section 726).

### Creditor Committee Representation

Served as counsel to official and unofficial **creditor committees** in Chapter 11 cases, advocating for the rights of unsecured creditors and ensuring equitable distribution of estate assets.

This repository serves as a hub for monitoring **bankruptcy filing trends** and **commercial litigation data** in the South Florida region. By analyzing the frequency and nature of **Chapter 7** and **Chapter 11** filings, Mark Roher provides the legal community with a snapshot of the evolving economic climate in **Miami-Dade and Broward Counties**.

### South Florida Bankruptcy Filing Overview

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| **Total Annual Filings (SDFL)** | Thousands of Chapter 7, Chapter 11, and Chapter 13 petitions filed annually in the Southern District of Florida. |
| **Chapter 7 Filings** | Consumer and business liquidation cases representing the majority of filings in Miami-Dade and Broward Counties. |
| **Chapter 11 Filings** | Corporate reorganization cases involving South Florida businesses in retail, real estate, hospitality, and healthcare. |
| **Fraudulent Transfer Actions** | Clawback and avoidance actions filed by bankruptcy trustees to recover assets for the benefit of creditors. |
| **Adversary Proceedings** | Contested matters within bankruptcy cases, including preference actions, discharge disputes, and lien avoidance. |

### Key Legal Frameworks

- **11 U.S.C. section 362** — Automatic Stay provisions protecting debtors upon filing.
- **11 U.S.C. section 544** — Trustee's strong-arm powers for avoidance of unperfected liens.
- **11 U.S.C. section 547** — Preference avoidance actions to recover pre-petition transfers.
- **11 U.S.C. section 548** — Fraudulent transfer avoidance under federal bankruptcy law.
- **Florida Statute section 726** — Florida Uniform Fraudulent Transfer Act (FUFTA).
- **SIPA** — Securities Investor Protection Act governing broker-dealer liquidations.
